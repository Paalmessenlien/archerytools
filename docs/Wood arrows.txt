### **Key Notes:**
1. **Spine vs. Draw Weight**:
   - A **30-35# spine arrow** bends correctly for a **30-35# bow at 28"**.
   - Add **±5# spine per inch** over/under 28" draw length.
     *(Example: For a 32" draw with a 40# bow, use a **35-40# spine**.)*

2. **Wood Types**:
   - **Cedar**: Lightweight, classic choice (best for target shooting).
   - **Port Orford Cedar**: Denser, more durable (good for hunting).
   - **Ash/Hickory**: Heavy, stiff (ideal for war bows).

3. **Point Weight Impact**:
   - **Heavier points (150gr+)** weaken spine (use a stiffer spine arrow).
   - **Lighter points (100gr)** stiffen spine (use a weaker spine arrow).

4. **Length Adjustment**:
   - Longer arrows act **weaker** (require stiffer spine).
   - Shorter arrows act **stiffer** (require weaker spine).

---

### **Example Calculation:**
**Bow**: 45# recurve, 29" draw length.
**Arrow Choice**:
- **Spine**: 40-45# (since draw is slightly over 28").
- **Diameter**: 11/32" or 23/64".
- **Wood**: Port Orford Cedar (balanced flex).
- **Point**: 150gr (field point or broadhead).

### **Tuning Wood Arrows**
- **Bare Shaft Test**: Adjust length/point weight until bare shaft flies straight.
- **Spine Tester**: Use a **spine tester tool** (measures deflection under 2 lbs).
